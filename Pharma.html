Pharmacist

- Pharmacists are medication experts who enhance patient care and promote wellness.
- A pharmacist is a health care professional specializing in the usage and administration of medication.
- A pharmacist, also known as a chemist (Commonwealth English) or a druggist (North America), is a healthcare professional who dispenses medications and who provides advice and guidance on their safe and effective use, with the aim of preventing disease and promoting public health.
- Pharmacists also contribute to research and testing of new drugs. They work in pharmacies, medical clinics, hospitals, universities, and government institutions.
- They can also provide information about general health topics like diet and exercise, as well as advice on products like home healthcare supplies and medical equipment.

List of Pharmacist Courses
D.Pharma

Diploma in Pharmacy is a two-year career-oriented diploma programme. Students interested in pursuing a long-term career in the medical field of pharmaceutical sciences and beginning a career in the pharmaceutical sector can enroll in the D Pharma programme.
D. Pharmacy course is the ideal choice for the student who wants a quick start in their pharmacy career. 
Diploma in Pharmacy covers fundamental Pharmacy Education including the application of chemistry in the pharma industry, theoretical & practical concepts of biochemistry, pharmacology and toxicology.
D.Pharmacy curriculum is designed to educate applicants to work in hospitals, community pharmacies, and other pharmaceutical-related industries under the supervision of a licenced pharmacist.
After obtaining a D.Pharma course degree the students have the scope to build a rewarding career for themselves in the pharmaceutical industry. The D.Pharma jobs after graduation include Clinical Pharmacist, Pharmacologist, Clinical Research Associate (CRA), and Junior Clinical Research Associate (JCRA), among others.

B.Pharma

B.Pharma (Bachelor of Pharmacy) is an undergraduate degree course in the area of pharmacy education. This course can be completed in 4 years by the science students after the 12th.
B.Pharma course consists of the study of a variety of topics related to pharmaceutical science, including drugs & medicines, medicinal chemistry, industrial pharmacy, and other topics.
Pharmacists can work in a range of hospitals as well as in various industries related to the prescription, manufacture & provision of medicines.
On completing the B Pharma course, students can work in the pharmaceutical business.
B.Pharmacy also talks about marketing of pharmaceutical products and patenting of new drugs.
